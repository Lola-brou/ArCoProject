Case Study: Okame Mask

Okame Mask – Museo delle Civiltà Collection

We chose the Okame mask as our case study because it perfectly encapsulated ArCo's metadata challenges while offering strong enrichment opportunities. Its combination of a generic label (“MASCHERA”) with distinctive characteristics (Japanese origin, folk theater use, and symbolic meaning) allowed us to demonstrate comprehensive improvements - from basic labeling to cultural contextualization. The mask's photographic documentation and museum provenance provided verifiable ground truth, making it an ideal test case for evaluating our LLM-assisted enrichment methodology across multiple semantic dimensions.

Retrieve Mask Items from ArCo + Choose Mask

This step is about exploring the ArCo knowledge graph and identifying gaps

Use a SPARQL query to list all cultural properties related to masks:

We began with a focused SPARQL query limited to Italian-language mask records to understand ArCo's baseline representation of these cultural artifacts. This first query revealed that most masks were labeled with generic terms like “MASCHERA” without cultural or functional specificity, highlighting a systemic metadata gap in the knowledge graph. The 50-result limit provided a manageable sample size to identify patterns while confirming the need for deeper analysis.

Mask-related Properties in English and Italian SPARQL Query

The second query broadened the scope to include both Italian and English labels, doubling the result limit to uncover more nuanced records. This approach revealed occasional specialized masks (like Chinese opera or Dayak tribal masks) that had richer descriptions, though most entries remained underspecified. Crucially, this bilingual search identified the Okame mask through its unique dc:subject field - a Japanese artifact with exceptional research potential due to its clear cultural identity amidst predominantly European mask records.

The current data is comprised of mostly administrative/technical metadata (catalog numbers, materials) treating cultural artifacts like inventory items. Our enrichments seek to add cultural semantics, human-readable descriptions, and multilingual accessibility.

We identified the following as important missing properties, seeking to propose new ownes or enrich existing ones to add critical cultural and contextual semantics

  • Typology - arco:hasTypology
  • Cultural context - arco:hasCulturalContext
  • Description - dc:description
  • More specific label - rdfs:label
